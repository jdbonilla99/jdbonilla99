About Me

I am a researcher with an MS in Engineering Sciences (Medical Devices) from the Thayer School of Engineering. I was a former King Scholar at Dartmouth College, where I earned a Bachelor of Arts (BA) in Biomathematics and Engineering and a Bachelor of Engineering (BE) in Biomaterials and Bioelectronics. My interests lie in design, health, and the intersection of technology and poverty alleviation.

Master's Research

My Master’s research focused on developing medical devices for children’s mental health, using human-centered design principles. This included integrating sensors in toys to monitor health metrics like steps, oxygen, heart rate, and more. These toys were developed as a therapeutic tool for children experiencing stress and depression, achieving high user satisfaction and accurate mood prediction.
