Projects

3D-Printed Arm Prostheses for LMICs: Designed affordable, functional arm prostheses for low- and middle-income countries (LMICs), with a focus on regions such as Honduras. Using 3D printing technology and inexpensive materials like polypropylene and thermoplastics, the prostheses offer a customizable solution to individuals with arm amputations.

ENGS 33 Bridge Design Project: Designed a truss bridge as part of a solid mechanics course to test structural efficiency and material usage. This project focused on using wood as the primary material, analyzing the strength-to-weight ratio and optimizing design for rural construction.

ENGS 31 UART Calculator Project: This project involved the development of a UART-based calculator using a Basys 3 board and a PS/2 keyboard. The calculator accepts keyboard inputs for performing basic arithmetic operations like addition, displaying results on a 7-segment display.

Laparoscopic Probe for Prostate Cancer Margin Detection: Developed a laparoscopic bioimpedance probe designed to detect positive surgical margins in prostate cancer patients during laparoscopic prostatectomy. This probe provides real-time feedback to the surgeon, reducing the likelihood of leaving positive margins and decreasing postoperative complications.

Point-of-Care Cervical Cancer HPV Screening Device: Developed a low-cost, point-of-care device aimed at improving cervical cancer screening, particularly in low-resource settings. The device utilizes a microfluidic platform to detect the presence of high-risk HPV strains.

Gold Nanoparticle-Enhanced Radiation Therapy for Cancer: Developed a novel treatment for pancreatic cancer using gold nanoparticles functionalized with P1G4 antibodies. These nanoparticles target pancreatic tumors and enhance the effects of radiation therapy by localizing radiation doses and amplifying biological effects.

Sweat-Based Glucose Monitor: Developed a non-invasive glucose monitoring device that uses sweat as a medium to measure glucose levels, eliminating the need for blood samples. This project focused on affordability and ease of use.

NiFe Permanent Magnets for Electric Motors and Turbines: Investigated the properties of NiFe (Nickel-Iron) permanent magnets for potential applications in electric motors and wind turbines. These magnets hold promise for large-scale implementation in renewable energy applications.

Bottles, Bins, and Beyond (Recycling Contamination Reduction): Developed a recycling bin system aimed at reducing contamination in beverage container recycling. The bin uses a gravity-based trapdoor mechanism to differentiate between contaminated and uncontaminated containers based on weight.
